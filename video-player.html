When the Rebbe assumed leadership of Chabad-Lubavitch in 1951, the Jewish world was still reeling from the devastation of the Holocaust. Traditional Jewish life in Europe had been decimated, and many wondered if Judaism could survive in the modern world.

The Rebbe's answer was revolutionary: rather than retreating into insularity, he advocated for bold outreach. "Every Jew is precious," he would often say, and this principle guided everything he did.
